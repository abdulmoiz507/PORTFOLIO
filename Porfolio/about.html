Who I Am

Background

I am Abdul Moiz, a passionate Cybersecurity student at MAJU University, currently in my first semester. Growing up in Karachi, I developed a deep curiosity for how technology works under the hood — not just how to use software, but how it is built, how it can be broken, and how it can be secured. That curiosity is what led me to pursue a formal degree in Cybersecurity.

Before entering university, I studied at The Educators, where I built a solid academic foundation. I was always drawn to mathematics, logic, and computers — subjects that directly feed into the technical demands of a career in digital security.

What I'm Exploring

As a first-semester student, I am in the exciting phase of exploring many different areas of technology at once. I have been learning the fundamentals of web development through HTML and CSS, understanding how web pages are structured and styled. Alongside that, I have started writing programs in C++, which is teaching me the core principles of programming logic — variables, loops, conditionals, and functions.

I have also experimented with Python, using it to build a simple number-guessing game that introduced me to user input, random number generation, and basic game loops. On the creative side, I used the Scratch editor to design an interactive game, which helped me think about user experience and event-driven programming in a visual way.

Beyond coding, I am interested in digital marketing concepts, graphic design basics, and understanding how businesses and individuals build their online presence — all of which connect to the security challenges they face in the modern digital landscape.

My Goals

My long-term goal is to become a skilled Cybersecurity professional — someone who can protect systems, investigate threats, and build secure digital infrastructure. In the short term, I am focused on mastering the fundamentals: programming, networking concepts, operating systems, and web technologies.

I believe in consistent, incremental progress. Every day I aim to learn something new, write some code, and work on a project that challenges me. This portfolio is a direct result of that mindset — a beginner's work, but built with genuine effort and a clear direction.
